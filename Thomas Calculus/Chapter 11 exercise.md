31. Average value If $f$ is continuous, the average value of the polar coordinate $r$ over the curve $r = f(\theta), \alpha \le \theta \le \beta$, with respect to $\theta$ is given by the formula   
    $$
    r_{av} = \dfrac{1}{\beta-\alpha}\int_{\alpha}^{\beta}f(\theta)d\theta
    $$   
    Use this formula to ind the average value of $r$ with respect to $\theta$ over the following curves $(a > 0)$.  
    a. The cardioid $r = a(1 - \cos \theta)$  
    b. The circle $r = a$  
    c. The circle $r = a \cos \theta,-\dfrac{\pi}{2} \le \theta \le \dfrac{\pi}{2}$   
    a. $\bar{r} = \dfrac{1}{\pi}\int_{-\frac{\pi}{2}}^{\frac{\pi}{2}}a(1-\cos\theta)d\theta$   
    $= \dfrac{a}{\pi}[\theta - \sin\theta]_{-\frac{\pi}{2}}^{\frac{\pi}{2}}$  
    $= \dfrac{a}{\pi}(\dfrac{\pi}{2}-1 +\dfrac{\pi}{2}-1)$  
    $= a - \dfrac{2a}{\pi}$   
    b. $\bar{r} = \dfrac{1}{\pi}\int_{-\frac{\pi}{2}}^{\frac{\pi}{2}}ad\theta$   
    $= \dfrac{a}{\pi}\theta]_{-\frac{\pi}{2}}^{\frac{\pi}{2}}$  
    $= a$   
    c. $\bar{r} = \dfrac{1}{\pi}\int_{-\frac{\pi}{2}}^{\frac{\pi}{2}}a\cos\theta d\theta$

#### Theory and Examples
69. If lines are drawn parallel to the coordinate axes through a point $P$ on the parabola $y^2 = kx, k > 0$, the parabola partitions the rectangular region bounded by these lines and the coordinate axes into two smaller regions, $A$ and $B$.  
a. If the two smaller regions are revolved about the $y$-axis, show that they generate solids whose volumes have the ratio 4:1.  
b. What is the ratio of the volumes generated by revolving the regions about the $x$-axis?   
![](../images/Thomas%20Calculus/11-4.jpg)    
a. $\dfrac{V_A}{V_B}= \dfrac{\int_0^{y_P}\frac{y^4}{k^2}dy}{\int_0^{y_P}x_P^2 - \frac{y^4}{k^2}dy}$  
$= \dfrac{\frac{1}{5k^2}y^5]_0^{y_P}}{[\frac{y_P^4}{k^2}y-\frac{1}{5k^2}y^5]_0^{y_P}}$  
$= \dfrac{\frac{1}{5k^2}y_P^5}{\frac{y_P^5}{k^2} - \frac{1}{5k^2}y_P^5}$  
$= \dfrac{y_P^5}{5k^2}\dfrac{5k^2}{5y_P^5-y_P^3}$  
$= \dfrac{1}{4}$  
b. $\dfrac{V_A}{V_B}= \dfrac{\int_0^{x_P}y_P^2-kxdx}{\int_0^{x_P}kxdx}$  
$= \dfrac{[kx_Px-\frac{k}{2}x^2]_0^{x_P}}{[\frac{k}{2}x^2]_0^{x_P}}$  
$= \dfrac{kx_P^2-\frac{k}{2}x_P^2}{\frac{kx_P^2}{2}}$  
$= 1$
